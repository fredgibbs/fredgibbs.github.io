# Designing an AI-Assisted Assignment

## Overview
For this assignment you will design a short learning exercise that uses AI to help you learn something you previously struggled to understand.

The topic should come from a class you have taken (or are currently taking). It might be a concept, skill, or type of problem that you never fully understood the first time around.

Instead of simply asking AI to explain the concept, your task is to **design a transparent learning process** that shows how AI can support real learning and understanding.

In other words, you are not just using AI to produce something. You are designing an **assignment for yourself** that demonstrates how AI can be used productively as a learning tool.


## Why We Are Doing This
Many students already use AI tools to complete assignments. But using AI to **finish work** is not the same as using AI to **learn something difficult**.

This project asks you to experiment with a different question:

> What would it look like to use AI to actually help someone learn?

Your assignment should make that learning process visible.

Someone else will need to follow your instructions and use AI to learn the same concept. We'll test this out in class!

## Bigger Picture
With your permission, these examples will be posted on the Amaranth AI craft circle webpage, which tries to provide faculty with ways to think about how to incorporate AI into learning processes more generally. Help make UNM a place with the best learners!


## What You Will Create
You will design a short AI-assisted learning exercise that includes:

1. A clear explanation of the concept or skill you want to learn
2. Step-by-step instructions for how AI should be used during the learning process
3. A demonstration of the learning activity
4. Evidence that the learning actually happened

Think of the final product as something like a **learning guide** or **instructional walkthrough**.


## Choosing a Topic
Your topic should be something that was genuinely difficult for you (and maybe continues to be).

Examples might include:
- a statistical concept
- a historical idea
- a programming concept
- a mathematical method
- a chemistry experiment
- a difficult reading from another class

The goal is not to choose something easy. The goal is to explore how AI might help with **challenging learning situations**.


## Designing the Learning Process
Your assignment should include clear instructions for how AI will be used.

These instructions should explain:
- what kinds of prompts should be used
- how the responses should be evaluated
- how the student should check whether the explanation is correct
- what activities help move from explanation to understanding

AI can help with things like:
- generating examples
- explaining concepts in different ways
- role playing for different perspecives
- creating practice exams that test and facilitate learning
- suggesting alternate explanations
- suggesting what might be missing


## Transparency
Your process should be transparent. This means showing:
- the prompts you used
- the responses you received
- how you evaluated those responses
- what worked and what did not
- how you **iteratively** improved your work

Learning with AI involves trial and error to learn how to guide it. I give you all the examples and hints I can, but there's no substitute for going through the process yourself and aligning your AI use to how you learn. Showing that process is part of the assignment.


## Reflection
Finally, include a short (~250 word) summary reflection discussing what you learned about learning with AI.

You might consider questions such as:
- What _kinds_ of prompts were most helpful?
- What patterns did you notice?
- Where did AI explanations fall short?
- What strategies helped turn AI responses into understanding?
- What surprised you about the process?

The goal of this reflection is to think about **how AI can augment the process of learning**.


## Assignment checklist

### Clear Learning Goal

The assignment clearly identifies a specific concept or skill that needed improvement.

### Well-Designed Learning Activity
The exercise provides clear and structured instructions for how AI should be used. Someone else should be able to follow the activity and attempt the same learning process.


### Thoughtful Use of AI
AI is used to support learning rather than simply produce answers. The project demonstrates awareness of both the strengths and limitations of AI tools.


### Evidence of Learning
The project shows clear evidence that the concept or skill was better understood by the end of the exercise.Readers should be able to see how the student's understanding changed.


### Process Transparency
The project documents how AI was used, including prompts and responses. The learning process should be visible rather than hidden.


### Reflection on the Learning Process
The reflection thoughtfully considers what worked, what did not, and what this experiment suggests about learning with AI. The goal is not to produce perfect conclusions, but to think seriously about the process.
